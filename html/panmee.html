HOW TO MAKE PAN MEE

JASON YAP

INTRODUCTION

Greetings everyone! This recipe provides detailed steps to making Pan Mee which is a Malaysian Chinese recipe that includes anchovy rich broth paired with hand-torn noodles made from scratch and topped with stir fried pork and shiitake mushrooms. It does require more time compared to your everyday meal. However, if you are an anchovy fan, this homemade recipe is mouthwatering.

COOK TIME 2 HOURS

Adjust Serving Size:

3

Base recipe is for 3 servings

INGREDIENTS

MAIN INGREDIENTS

1 bunch of Yu Choy, cleaned and prewashed
Vegetable oil, for frying
150 grams (g) dried anchovy, heads removed

ANCHOVY SOUP BASE

24 cups (6 quarts) water
2 pounds (lbs.) pork bones
0.5 lb. dried anchovy
1 large yellow onion, halves
1 teaspoon (tsp) fish sauce, to taste
1 tsp white peppercorn, crushed
1 thumb-size ginger, quartered
1 honey date

NOODLES/DOUGH

6 cups all-purpose flour
2 tablespoon (tbsp) vegetable oil
1 cup water + 1 cup water
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 tbsp cornstarch

GROUND PORK AND SHIITAKE TOPPING

1 lb. ground pork
10 pieces dried shiitake mushroom soaked + stems removed, keep mushroom juices!
5 cloves garlic, minced
1 shallot, minced
1 thumb-size ginger, minced
1 tsp chicken bouillon powder
1 tbsp fish sauce

DIPPING SAUCE (SPICY)

10 Thai chili-peppers, cut to small pieces
soy sauce, to taste

PREPARATION

Neck pork bones are my soup bone of choice, but any meat bones would do. Chicken bones are another popular choice. You can also omit the meat if you are in the umami mood.

Generally, you want to mince your topping ingredients, so they are subtle in your dish. However, I like to see what I am eating, and you can cut them to small pieces as well.

It is also a good time to dice Thai chili peppers and set them aside or in the fridge for more exciting taste.

INSTRUCTIONS

STEP 1: Preparing the Soup Base

Cooking the soup begins with rinsing pork bones under cold water while removing impurities. Soak and discard cloudy water. Repeat this step until water is clear as the goal is to make the soup clearer and see through.

STEP 2: Cooking the Soup

Bring 6 quarts of water to a boil while adding all the soup ingredients. Reduce heat to low and gently give a couple stirs. Close the lid and simmer for 2 hours or until the soup has its sweetness from the pork. Keeping the contents in the pot at a light simmer to reduce evaporation and ensuring flavor consistency. You can add more fish sauce or salt to taste.

STEP 3: Preparing the Dough

While this soup is in the work, prepare the dough by putting 6 cups of all-purpose flour into a bowl mixer or hand mixer. Add 1 cup of water along with the oil, salt, sugar and cornstarch and mix. Carefully add water or flour as needed and knead the dough until it is no longer sticky. Cover your bowl and let the dough rest for at least an hour.

STEP 4: Preparing the Pork and Mushroom Topping

Next, we prepare the ground pork and shiitake mushroom topping. Bring a non-stick pan or wok to a medium heat. Stir fry 1 lb. ground pork until all its liquids have evaporated. Once the pork begins to release its oils, add in the minced garlic, ginger, and shallots and mix. When the garlic is fragrant, add in the shiitake mushrooms, chicken bouillon, and fish sauce. Reduce to low heat and let simmer until its water has evaporated to your preference. Add water for a waterier consistency or combine hot water with cornstarch to create a more gravy like finish. Dish in a bowl and set aside for later.

STEP 5: Frying the Anchovies

In a frying pan, heat oil in medium heat. Once the oil is at least 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Put remaining 150g dried anchovies in and constantly stir. Note this will take about 10-15 minutes. Fry until anchovies are golden light brown and crispy. Strain the anchovies and place on top of a napkin on a plate to cool down.

STEP 6: Preparing the Noodles

In a large pot of water, put 1 tbsp vegetable oil, 1 tbsp salt and bring water to boil. Knead and tear the dough to smaller parts which can be held by both hands. There are many ways to prepare hand tear noodles. One way is to stretch the dough, flatten your dough to approximately those below. You can use a roller to flatten to your preference. Place the dough as you tear them into the heating water.

STEP 7: Cooking the Noodles and Vegetables

When all the dough is in the pot. Stir a couple of times to ensure none are sticking together. Once water is boiling and all the noodles have raised to the top, strain them and dish in a serving bowl. In the same water, boil leafy greens for 3-5 minutes or soft to your preference.

ASSEMBLY

STEP 1: Final Preparation

Heat the soup to a rolling boil and strain any of the impurities on top as shown in the photo.

STEP 2: Plating

In a bowl, place a portion of noodles, followed by topping and vegetables. Garnish with white pepper, sesame oil, and scallions to your preference.

STEP 3: Serving

Pour the hot soup over and garnish with crispy anchovies!

Serve immediately and top with more ingredients as everyone please!

Notes

  • Break the chilis to smaller pieces for a spicier taste.
  • Use plain soy sauce if you aren't a fan of spicy.
  • Recipe quantities automatically adjust based on your selected serving size.
